The Flask Backend Explained

This feedback form is powered by Flask, a simple Python framework. Here's how it works in plain English:

What Happens When You Submit?

  1. You fill the form with your name, email (optional), suggestion, and link (optional)
  2. You click Submit and the form sends your data to the backend
  3. Flask receives the data from your browser
  4. Flask checks everything - is the name filled? Is the email valid? Is the URL real?
  5. If valid, Flask saves it to a JSON file (a simple text file)
  6. Flask sends back a message saying "Success!" and your form shows the thank you message

Key Security Features

  • Email addresses must be real format (example@domain.com)
  • URLs must start with http:// or https://
  • Names can't be longer than 100 characters
  • Suggestions can't be longer than 5000 characters
  • All inputs are checked twice (once in your browser, once on the server)

What Happens to Your Data?

  • Status "pending" - Your feedback is saved and waiting for review
  • Admin approval - An admin reads and approves your feedback
  • Status "approved" - Your feedback is now visible on the site
  • File storage - Everything is saved in a feedback_data.json file
